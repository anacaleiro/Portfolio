Ana Pedro Caleiro Portfolio

Understanding customer behavior is essential for businesses in the tourism and hospitality industry to remain competitive. Hotel H, a Lisbon-based hotel, sought to refine its marketing strategy by transitioning from a basic segmentation model to a more comprehensive, data-driven approach. The data used in this analysis is from a real chain of portuguese hotels.

This project applied the CRISP-DM framework and leveraged K-Means clustering, alongside Self-Organizing Maps (SOMs) and DBSCAN, to segment customers based on geographic, demographic, and behavioral factors. Through this process, we identified distinct customer clusters, enabling the hotel to develop personalized marketing campaigns, tailored pricing strategies, and improved customer engagement initiatives.

By integrating an automated pipeline, the model ensures continuous updates as new data becomes available, making it a scalable solution for long-term business growth. The implementation of this segmentation strategy is expected to improve customer retention, optimize marketing costs, and drive revenue growth.
